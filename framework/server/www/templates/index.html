Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SEO, AEO, and our services.

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of optimizing your website to rank higher in search engine results pages like Google, Bing, and Yahoo. It involves improving technical aspects of your site, creating high-quality content, building authoritative backlinks, and ensuring a great user experience — all to increase organic (non-paid) traffic to your website.

AI Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of optimizing your website content to be cited and recommended by AI-powered platforms like ChatGPT, Google SGE, Perplexity, and Claude. Unlike traditional SEO which focuses on ranking in search results, AEO focuses on making your content the preferred source that AI systems reference when answering user questions.

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) focuses on ranking your pages in traditional search results. AEO (AI Engine Optimization) focuses on getting your content cited by AI assistants and AI-powered search features. In 2026, a complete digital visibility strategy requires both — search engines still drive the majority of traffic, while AI platforms are rapidly growing as a discovery channel.

Most clients see measurable improvements in organic traffic within 90 days. Significant results typically appear between 3-6 months, depending on your starting point, competition level, and the scope of optimization. Our Growth plan clients see an average 300% traffic increase within 12 months.

Core Web Vitals are a set of Google metrics that measure real-world user experience on your website. They include Largest Contentful Paint (loading speed), Interaction to Next Paint (interactivity), and Cumulative Layout Shift (visual stability). These metrics directly impact your search rankings and are a key part of our technical SEO optimization.

Technical SEO refers to optimizations made to your website's infrastructure to help search engines crawl, index, and render your pages more effectively. This includes improving site speed, fixing broken links, implementing proper URL structures, adding structured data markup, ensuring mobile-friendliness, and optimizing your XML sitemap and robots.txt file.

On-page SEO involves optimizing individual web pages to rank higher in search results. This includes optimizing title tags, meta descriptions, header tags, image alt text, internal linking, and the content itself. The goal is to make each page clearly relevant to specific search queries while providing genuine value to visitors.

Off-page SEO refers to actions taken outside your website to improve its authority and rankings. The most important factor is building high-quality backlinks from reputable websites. It also includes brand mentions, social signals, digital PR, and building your overall online reputation. Off-page SEO signals tell search engines that your site is trustworthy and authoritative.

Backlinks are links from other websites that point to your site. They act as "votes of confidence" in the eyes of search engines. The more high-quality, relevant backlinks your site earns, the more authoritative it appears, which directly improves your rankings. Not all backlinks are equal — links from reputable, relevant websites carry far more weight than low-quality ones.

Local SEO is the practice of optimizing your online presence to attract customers from local searches. This includes optimizing your Google Business Profile, building local citations, managing reviews, and ensuring your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) information is consistent across the web. It's essential for businesses that serve customers in a specific geographic area.

Keyword research is the process of identifying the search terms your target audience uses when looking for products, services, or information related to your business. We analyze search volume, competition, intent, and relevance to build a strategic keyword map that guides your content creation and on-page optimization efforts.

Structured data is code added to your website that helps search engines understand your content more precisely. Schema markup is the most common format, and it can generate rich results like star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, event details, and product information directly in search results. It also plays a critical role in AEO by helping AI models accurately interpret your content.

We provide transparent monthly reports with clear metrics tied directly to your business goals.

Traditional content marketing focuses on creating content that attracts and engages human readers through search and social channels. AEO goes a step further by structuring content so that AI systems can easily parse, understand, and cite it. This means optimizing for entity recognition, factual accuracy, clear definitions, and authoritative sourcing — ensuring AI platforms recommend your business.

Topical authority is a measure of how comprehensively your website covers a particular subject area. Search engines and AI models favor websites that demonstrate deep expertise across related topics rather than sites with scattered, unrelated content. We build topical authority through strategic content clusters that cover every aspect of your core subjects.
